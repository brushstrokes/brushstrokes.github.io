How to String a Guitar

With Pictures!

By Gilbert Salas



Introduction

So you'd like to restring your electric guitar -- but don't know how! Here is a short tutorial of some tips and tricks I've learned over the years of playing guitar. There are many varieties and flavours of guitar, strings, hardware that is available on the market. So fundamentally -- this is going work more so as a guideline than a specific tutorial. I cannot guarentee that this arrangement of steps is appropriate or suitable for your particular setups. If you have any questions, comments, queries, or concerns; please see my 'Contact Information' section located at the bottom of this page.


Items You May Need

  • String winder
  • Microfiber cloth
  • Guitar
  • Set of six strings (any gauge will do)
  • Wire cutter
  • Guitar tuner

Instructions

  1. Remove your old strings with care!

    2. Please be mindful to loosen the strings first using the tuning pegs. Cutting tight strings with a wire cutter could cause a string to whip wildly, causing possible harm or danger.

  2. Clean your guitar as needed, here is a list of some parts you may want to clean:
    • The body
    • The headstock
    • Tuning pegs

    You're going to need a clean microfiber cloth to prevent any damage to delicate wood.

  3. Once your guitar is cleaned and the old strings are removed. Open your new package of strings.

    4. Strings come in all sorts of varieties. Make sure that you buy the appropriate strings for your guitar. Acoustic and electric guitar strings different not only by material, but by gauge. The package that your strings come inside of will indicate the order in which the strings belong. They typically describe this by a particular color on one end. For your convenience, here is a list of different types of guitar string options:

  4. Run your first string over the fretboard up and through the first tuning peg on your guitar. Make sure that you give the string some extra slack and not to pull it too tightly through the post. After that bend the string around the tuning peg so it stays in place. From there, you're able to wind the string with your hand or the string winder(which is much easier). Be cautious not to wind the string to tightly. You do not need to tune the string yet. Make it just tight enough to have slight tension when you pluck it.

  5. Proceed to repeat step four (4) for the remaining number of strings. For this example we will are demonstrating on a standard six string guitar. If you are using any other number of strings then this tutorial is notappropriate for you.

  6. After you have strung up all of the string, tighten them up a little bit to create more tension. They still don't have to be tuned, you're going to stretch the string out. Since a machine wind ups the string when it's manufactured, you need to stretch them out initially to make them just right. It is usually best to go down each fret one at a time with your left hand(right hand if you're a lefty) and just tug on the string.

  7. Once you feel you have initally stretched the strings out, you are finally ready to tun the guitar up. Note that these are the standard note tunings:
    • E
    • A
    • D
    • G
    • B
    • e

  8. You're good to go! Enjoy your freshly strung and tuned guitar! :-)
